What I found

The 3 bottlenecks between your senior-only model and the next tier of revenue.

Your founder-level execution is the product. Senior capacity, a reputation-dependent pipeline, and proposal drag are the three constraints in the way.

Bottleneck 1

Senior team capacity is the ceiling on growth

With a 4-person team and a senior-only model, every new retainer means more hours from the same people. The work that makes Salient different, founder-level strategy and pitching, is also the work that does not scale linearly.

The math: if each retainer requires 15 to 20 hours a week of senior attention and you run 6 to 8 active accounts, that is 90 to 160 hours a week across a 4-person team. Add business development, admin, and internal ops and the math stops working past 8 accounts without diluting quality.
Bottleneck 2

New business pipeline dependent on reputation with no systematic outreach

Your work speaks for itself, but waiting for inbound referrals means you cannot control the timing or volume of new business. When a retainer ends and nothing is behind it, revenue dips.

The math: a single new B2B tech PR retainer at $15K/mo adds $180K a year. If a systematic outreach engine surfaces even 2 new retainers a quarter that reputation alone would not have, that is $360K in incremental annual revenue.
Bottleneck 3

Proposal customization consuming founder time

No templates means every proposal is built from scratch. That is part of what makes Salient's pitches win, but it also means founder hours are spent on pre-sales work that may not convert.

The math: if each custom proposal takes 8 to 12 hours and you pursue 4 to 6 new opportunities a quarter, that is 32 to 72 hours of founder time, roughly 1 to 2 full work weeks, spent on business development.
