What I found

The 3 workflow bottlenecks I found.

Your dental expertise is the product. Manual reporting, hand-built benchmarking, and a referral-only pipeline are the three constraints in the way.

Bottleneck 1

Manual Production and Collections Reporting

Dental CFO work requires pulling production data by provider, calculating collections rates against fee schedules, tracking insurance reimbursements, and building overhead breakdowns by category. Each client's monthly package requires hours of manual data extraction and formatting.

The math: 5 to 8 hours per client per month on reporting. With 10 clients, that is 50 to 80 hours a month on deliverables alone.
Bottleneck 2

Practice-Level Benchmarking Done by Hand

Every dental practice owner wants to know how their overhead ratios, provider production, and collections rates compare to industry benchmarks. Building those comparisons manually for each client each month is repetitive work that does not scale.

The math: 2 to 3 hours per client per month on benchmarking. That is 20 to 30 hours a month across a 10-client roster.
Bottleneck 3

Referral-Only Client Acquisition

Dental is a tight community, and most dental-focused CFO practices grow through dentist-to-dentist referrals. That caps your pipeline. You have no systematic way to surface practices hiring associates, opening locations, or making equipment investments before they start looking for financial help.

The math: one new client per quarter from referrals versus 2 to 3 from systematic outreach. At your engagement size, that is $40K to $120K in missed annual revenue.
