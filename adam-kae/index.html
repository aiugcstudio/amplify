Each retainer needs the same core read: revenue by client, delivery cost, utilization, service-line margin, pricing gaps, cash forecast, and owner decisions. By hand that eats 10 to 20 hours per client per month, so capacity is capped by production instead of judgment.

The math: drop that to 4 to 7 hours of review and you get 6 to 13 hours back per client per month. Across several retainers plus research and proposals, that's the path to 30+ hours a week returned.
Owners aren't paying you for a dashboard. They're paying for the read: which clients are profitable, which services are underpriced, and what to do next.
Bottleneck 2

Pricing and margin pain is not captured at the buying moment

Owners get ready for CFO help in narrow windows: headcount jumps, a new service launches, a retainer goes unprofitable, or revenue rises while cash doesn't. Those events are public more often than people think.

The math: a signal-led engine that surfaces 8 to 12 high-fit owners a month, converting even a small fraction into 2 to 4 annual retainers, adds $100K to $250K at your $4K/month-plus economics.

A $4K/month-plus retainer feels discretionary as monthly advice. It becomes an economic decision only when the proposal shows client-margin leakage, underpriced services, and founder hours lost to finance cleanup.
