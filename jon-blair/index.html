What I found

The 3 workflow bottlenecks I found.

Your strategic advisory is the product. The recurring production work, the referral-dependent pipeline, and the repeated onboarding are the three constraints in the way.

Bottleneck 1

Every $10M+ client needs the same inventory-to-cash model

DTC founders at $10M+ all hit the same cash crisis: revenue is growing but free cash flow is negative because inventory purchases, ad spend, and retail terms all hit at different times. You rebuild the same inventory-to-cash model for every client. The framework is identical.

The math: 5 hours per client per month across 50 clients is 250 hours a month. Even with your team handling half, that's 125 hours of senior review. At $200/hr, that's $300K a year on the same financial visibility every DTC founder lacks.
Bottleneck 2

Your buyers hire in a crisis, not on a schedule

DTC founders don't hire a fractional CFO when things are going well. They hire when they can't make payroll despite record revenue, or when their Amazon margins go negative and they don't know why. The podcast builds trust over months. The crisis hits in a week.

The math: one quiet quarter without new sign-ups costs $40K to $80K in deferred retainer revenue. A signal-driven engine surfaces the founders who just raised, just launched on Amazon, or just signed a retail deal that's about to wreck their cash flow.
Bottleneck 3

Onboarding rediscovers the same architecture every time

Every DTC brand thinks the channel mix (Shopify vs. Amazon vs. wholesale vs. retail) makes their business unique. But the financial architecture underneath is the same: contribution margin by channel, CAC by cohort, inventory turns, and cash conversion cycle. The onboarding shouldn't take 10 hours to discover that.

The math: 6 to 10 hours per onboarding across 15 to 20 new clients a year is 90 to 200 hours. Cutting that to under 2 hours frees over a month of senior time for the advisory work that retains clients.
