What I found

The 3 workflow bottlenecks I found.

Your planning expertise is the product. A network-dependent pipeline, custom proposals, and from-scratch documentation are the three constraints in the way.

Bottleneck 1

Business development relies on your personal network

New engagements come from your reputation and relationships built over decades. That works, but it caps growth at the reach of one person's network and leaves deal flow lumpy. The manufacturers signaling a planning need right now, the ones you don't already know, never make it onto your radar.

The math: a signal desk that surfaces 8 to 12 high-tech manufacturers a month at their planning-system moment, converted conservatively, adds a run of new engagements a year, the path to 30 to 50% more revenue.
Bottleneck 2

Proposals require deep customization

Every manufacturer thinks their planning problem is unique. The proposal has to show you understand their volume, product mix, and maturity. But the scoping framework is the same each time, and the hours go into formatting and tailoring, not insight.

The math: cutting proposal turnaround from days to under 2 hours across every pitch frees weeks of senior selling time a year and stops warm leads going cold before the scope lands.
Bottleneck 3

Implementation documentation is created from scratch every time

Every engagement produces process documentation, workflow maps, and runbooks. The structure is 90% identical across manufacturers, but each write-up starts from a blank page, and it's senior time spent formatting instead of advising.

The math: 15 hours of documentation per engagement across a full slate is 120 to 150 hours of senior time a year on write-ups. Drafting it for a review pass instead of a rebuild hands most of that time back.
